# THE VEILWRACK — GM SECRETS
**Do not reveal to players. Reveal through play.**

## The Truth

The Anemoi — the sky-leviathans — did not merely ride the wind. **They made
it.** The Breath of the Veilwrack was the collective exhalation of the living
leviathans: a self-renewing atmosphere of intentful air, generated the way a
forest generates oxygen.

One thousand years ago there was one Anemo left: **Suruveil**, the Worldlung,
ancient and slowing. The ancestors of the modern Alar — roostless then,
overcrowded on the few dead husks that existed — hunted Suruveil for three
generations and killed it. They settled its corpse. It is the capital. The
**Founding Compact**, sealed in the Spirarchy's Tenth Gallery, is the treaty
the three kindreds signed over the cooling body: the Vael guided the hunt,
the Roak engineered the killing harpoons, the Ossuin sang it to sleep. All
three founding lineages are complicit, which is why all three institutions
keep the secret.

The wind has been dying ever since — slowly at first, a fat inheritance spent
over forty generations. The Stillings are not an invasion. **They are the
bottom of the barrel.** The Breath is running out, and where it runs out
first, the air simply stops being alive.

### The Hushed
An Alar trapped in a Stilling for more than a day undergoes the **Quieting**:
the living breath in their body — the same breath in spire-marrow — is drawn
out into the vacuum of the dead zone. What walks out is animate but exhales
stillness (every Hushed slowly extends the Stilling around itself). They are
not evil; they are *holes*. They seek out moving air and crowds the way the
starving seek bread. The Hushed Choir cult is partially right: the Hushed can
be communicated with, by sign and by writ, and they remember who they were.

### The Hope
Anemoi spawned in the deep cloud-sea. The Ossuin funeral chants are literal:
the dead are carried down as *payment*, a custom older than the Compact's
secrecy, because the priesthood has always known the debt. Somewhere below
the Undermist's surface drift dormant **leviathan eggs** — the Ossuin call
them the Unsung. The Undermist is survivable with preparation (breath-bottles
of spire-marrow gas, cold-wax feather seal); the real dangers are pressure,
blindness, murk-predators, and the things the Hushed Choir sent down ahead
of you. Waking an Unsung requires Breath — a great deal of it, willingly
exhaled. A city's worth of lungs, or one bottled spire's marrow, or the
voluntary final breath of every member of a Warden company. The math is the
campaign's moral engine: **the wind can be reborn, but only by giving back
what was taken.**

## Campaign Arc (suggested)

1. **Rescue at Lanternfall (intro).** A roost-spire's windlane is Stilling.
   Evacuation under pressure; first contact with a Stillwight; a Hushed child
   follows the PCs out — it remembers its mother.
2. **The Sealed Gallery.** The Wardens need the Spirarchy's suppressed survey
   charts. Archive intrigue in the Crown; the Quillate's High Quill becomes
   ally or enemy; discovery of the millennium hole in the Record.
3. **Greywake.** Expedition to the drowned Spire. The Hushed roost there in
   their hundreds — not as monsters, but as a congregation, *waiting*. Found:
   a Compact-era killing harpoon, still lodged in the spire-bone, and the
   first whole copy of the Founding Compact.
4. **The Ossuin Silence.** Why the death-priests stopped diving: their last
   diver returned saying the Unsung have begun to *hatch hungry*, and there
   is nothing down there to feed them. Decision point: feed the eggs, or let
   them die and keep the bottled marrow for the living Spires.
5. **The Worldlung Debt (finale).** Waking an Unsung over the heart of the
   oldest Stilling. Every faction converges. Someone must pay the Breath-debt.

## Stilling Mechanics (GM reference)

- Flight inside: Flight roll (Hard) per round or +1 Fatigue; no gliding.
- Hearing-Perception: Herculean. Windworking: impossible.
- Quieting: after 24h inside, opposed roll Willpower vs Stilling Potency
  (oldest zones: 80; new fringes: 50) once per day. Two consecutive losses =
  Hushed.
- A Stilling grows ~100m/season; doubles in speed for every Hushed dwelling
  in it; *recedes* measurably around a working Windworker who spends MP daily
  (the cult knows this; it is why some of their singers survive).

## NPC Quick List

- **Warden-Commander Esk Veil-Torn** (Vael, f, grizzled): PCs' commander.
  Lost her wing-sister at Greywake. Passion: Recover the Greywake dead 80%.
- **High Quill Orrocan** (Roak, m, ancient): knows the Record's hole exists,
  terrified of what un-sealing it does to Roak legitimacy. Potential ally.
- **Spirarch Velute the Ninth** (Roak, f): not a villain — a custodian of a
  lie she inherited and believes necessary. Will sacrifice the Wardens to
  preserve order; will sacrifice herself to preserve the Crown.
- **Cantor Hssh** (Hushed, formerly Ossuin): the Choir's interlocutor. Writes
  beautifully. Wants the PCs to understand the Stilling is "the world
  breathing in."
- **Brell Coinfeather** (Vael, m): Marrower magnate. Has the suppressed sink
  data. Buyable, once. Genuinely funny; genuinely amoral.
